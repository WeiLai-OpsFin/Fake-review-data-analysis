# Key Findings

## 1. Fake Reviews Mainly Operate Through Authenticity and Trust

The fake-review condition substantially reduces perceived review authenticity. The real-minus-fake difference in `ReviewAuthenticity` is 0.746, with p = 0.0000. Review authenticity is then strongly associated with trust, with a path coefficient of 0.749.

## 2. Trust Is the Strongest Direct Predictor of Booking Intention

The path from `Trust` to `BookingIntention` is 0.457, with p = 2.633e-05. This supports the interpretation that fake reviews matter because they weaken the trust-building function of online reviews.

## 3. DID Evidence Is Exploratory

The DID-style estimate for booking intention is -0.130, with p = 0.412. This result is not statistically significant in the small pilot sample, so the DID component is used as a transparent comparison rather than the primary evidence.

## 4. The SEM Model Has Strong Explanatory Power

The PLS-SEM-style model explains 66.7% of the variance in booking intention. This is useful because it documents a complete empirical workflow: construct design, measurement checks, structural paths, mediation, and interpretation.
